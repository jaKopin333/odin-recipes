Borscht With Sour Cream

Home

Description

Halushki, a popular dish of traditional Ukrainian dumplings, consists of plump flour-based dumplings tossed with pan-fried bacon and veggies. Typically, it's served in Ukrainian cafes at lunch or dinner time alongside a light salad.

Below, find a high-level overview of the flavor, texture, and cooking time.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 medium beets, peeled and grated
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and grated
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 small cabbage, shredded
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 2 tbsp fresh dill, chopped

Steps

  1. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add 1 large diced onion and cook for 5 minutes until translucent.
  3. Add 2 minced garlic cloves and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in 4 grated beets and 2 grated carrots, cooking for 5 minutes to soften.
  5. Pour in 6 cups vegetable broth and bring to a boil.
  6. Add 2 cubed potatoes and 1/2 shredded cabbage.
  7. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30 minutes until vegetables are tender.
  8. Stir in 2 tbsp red wine vinegar, 1 tsp salt, and 1/2 tsp black pepper.
  9. Ladle into bowls and top each with a dollop of 1/2 cup sour cream.
  10. Garnish with 2 tbsp chopped fresh dill.
